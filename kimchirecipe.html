KIMCHI RECIPE

Ingrediants

1 medium Napa cabbage 1/4 cup coarse sea salt 4 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon grated ginger 2 tablespoons Korean red pepper flakes (gochugaru) 2 tablespoons fish sauce or soy sauce (for vegan option) 2 tablespoons sugar 4 green onions, chopped 1 medium carrot, julienned 1 daikon radish, julienned (optional)

Recipe

Cut the Napa cabbage into quarters lengthwise and remove the core. Cut each quarter crosswise into 2-inch pieces. Place the cabbage pieces in a large bowl and sprinkle salt between the leaves. Toss gently to distribute the salt evenly. Let it sit for about 2 hours, tossing occasionally. Rinse the cabbage under cold water to remove excess salt. Drain well and set aside. In a separate bowl, mix garlic, ginger, red pepper flakes, fish sauce (or soy sauce), and sugar to make the kimchi paste. Add green onions, carrot, and daikon radish (if using) to the paste and mix well. Rub the kimchi paste mixture onto each cabbage leaf, making sure to coat them thoroughly. Pack the cabbage tightly into a clean jar or airtight container, pressing down to remove any air bubbles. Leave about an inch of space at the top of the jar, then seal tightly. Let the kimchi ferment at room temperature for 1-2 days, then transfer it to the refrigerator. Kimchi will continue to ferment in the fridge, developing its flavor over time. It can be enjoyed for several weeks.

Tips

Use gloves when handling the red pepper flakes to avoid skin irritation. Fermentation time may vary depending on the temperature and your taste preferences. Taste the kimchi after 1 day of fermentation and adjust the fermentation time accordingly.
